figure 01 · the boundary, softly drawn

Somewhere between Delancey and Rivington, tucked under the long shadow of the Williamsburg Bridge, sits a four-block secret. Locals have walked it forever. It never had a name. Now it does.

C.A.R.P. — for the four streets that hold it in: Clinton, Attorney, Ridge, Pitt. A pocket neighborhood the size of a good long walk, thick with fire escapes, bodegas, tenement iron, and the low hum of the bridge overhead.

Say it out loud. It's already yours.
